About Kidney Disease Detection

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a condition where the kidneys gradually lose their function over time. It's a serious health issue that can lead to complications like high blood pressure, anemia, weak bones, and even kidney failure if not managed properly. Early detection and lifestyle changes, such as a healthy diet and regular exercise, play a crucial role in managing CKD and improving quality of life for those affected.


Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) represents a significant global health challenge, necessitating early detection and accurate diagnosis for effective management and treatment. Recent advancements in medical imaging and machine learning techniques, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), offer promising avenues for assisting healthcare professionals in CKD diagnosis. This project aims to leverage a dataset comprising CT images to develop a CNN-based model for automated CKD diagnosis. By extracting complex features from medical images, CNNs enable the identification of patterns indicative of CKD-related abnormalities, potentially improving diagnostic accuracy and patient outcomes. The project involves dataset pre-processing, CNN model implementation, training, evaluation, interpretation, and optimization to enhance CKD diagnosis.



Our project focuses on using advanced CNN technology to detect kidney diseases from CT images. We analyze various conditions including tumors, cysts, stones, and normal kidney health to provide accurate diagnoses.
