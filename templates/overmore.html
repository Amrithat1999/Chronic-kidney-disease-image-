Normal Kidney functions

The kidneys play a crucial role in maintaining overall health by filtering waste products, excess salts, and water from the blood to form urine. They also help regulate blood pressure, balance electrolytes, and produce hormones essential for red blood cell production. Healthy kidneys function efficiently to remove toxins while preserving essential nutrients in the body. Regular hydration, balanced diet, and regular physical activity support optimal kidney function and overall well-being.

Tumor

Kidney tumors, also known as renal cell carcinoma, are abnormal growths that develop in the kidneys. These tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Common symptoms of kidney tumors include blood in urine, persistent pain in the back or side, and a palpable mass in the abdomen. Diagnosis typically involves imaging tests like CT scans or MRIs, followed by biopsy for confirmation. Treatment options may include surgery, chemotherapy, or targeted therapy, depending on the tumor's type, size, and stage.

Cysts

Kidney cysts are fluid-filled sacs that can develop on the kidneys. They are generally non-cancerous and often do not cause symptoms. However, larger cysts or multiple cysts may lead to discomfort in the back or side, high blood pressure, or urinary tract infections. Diagnosis typically involves imaging tests like ultrasound or CT scans. Treatment is usually not necessary for simple kidney cysts unless they cause complications or symptoms. Regular monitoring through check-ups is recommended for larger or complex cysts.

Kidney Stones

Kidney stones are solid deposits that form in the kidneys from minerals and salts in urine. They can vary in size and may cause severe pain when passing through the urinary tract. Common symptoms include intense back or abdominal pain, nausea, and difficulty urinating. Factors such as dehydration, diet high in certain substances like calcium or oxalate, and family history can increase the risk of kidney stone formation. Treatment options range from pain management and hydration to procedures like lithotripsy or surgical removal for larger stones.
